BRIEF BIOGRAPHY OF OWU KUTA KINGDOM



THE SEVEN GRAND CHILDREN OF ODUDUWA

Accounts about the first descendants and therefore of Yoruba dynasties differ from place to place. In his account in “The History of the Yorubas” the distinguised historian and author, Samuel Johnson, enumerates the seven grandchildren of Oduduwa and founder of the SeniorYoruba Dynasties as:

(1) A Princess (IYUN ADE) who was the mother of Owu's
(2) Akinsua of Benin
(3) Orangun of Ila
(4) Onipopo of Popo
(5) The Onisabe of Sabe
(6) Alaketu of Ketu
(7) Orauyan of Oyo

GENERAL HISTORY OF OWU

The first Olowu of Owu, being the son of a Princess, derived his royalty by virtue of his maternal descent from Oduduwa and therefore acquired the right to wear a crown like his uncles in rather unusual circumstances. The story as recorded by Samuel Johnson is that when Olowu was a child, he often played on his grandfather’s laps while the old man wore his crown. In one such childish pranks, the child pulled at the crown and his indulgent grandfather placed it on his head much to the delight of the child. When attempts were made later to take the crown back, the child was said to have cried so much that the grandfather directed that the boy be allowed to keep it. Thus, the first Olowu came to,be called ASUNKUNGBADE (One who cried to secure a crown). This story is accepted by generations of surviving Own Communities to date that the first Olowu wasAsunkungbade, hence the name becoming one of the appelations (oriki) of all Owus. It is interesting to note that wherever Olowu’s children went to establish settlements, they went with Beaded Crown and are generally referred to as Olowus with the appelations (Omo) sons toAjibosin to indicate that they all have common ancestors “Oba Ajibosin”, founder and first Olowu ofOwu Dynasty.


SPECIFIC HISTORY OF OWU KUTA

lndigenes of this community under the leadership ofAkindele otherwise known as Anlugbua the direct son of the first Olowu (Ajibosin)migrated from Orile-Owu according to oral tradition around—the middle part of the fifteenth century. Akindele Anlugbua left Owu Ipole as a result of personal ambition to establish a kingdom for himself.

Akindele was one of the sons of the first Olowu of Own Ipole, Oba AjibosinAsunkungbade whose mother was Olawumi lyunade, a daughter of Oduduwa of Old Ife Dynasty. Akindele (Anlugbua) was a renowned warrior, a giant-like figure, a jujuman and a brave hunter. He fought several wars with tremendous success for Owu people with the historical consequence that Own people becametarget of envy and hatred of their neighbouring towns and villages. It was in one of such wars that his horse stepped on ‘skin’ peeled off from yarns and thus slipped down while it also fell on the cover of groundnuts. He brought with him to his kingdom the following:

The most coveted beaded crown
The bravest war horse
The most beautiful and strongest swords

He also came along with most powerful charms and means and of course his pregnant wife to found KUTA. Morolahun was the only son born to him. Morolahun became the first Olowu ofKuta and was blessed with five children: a female and four males from which fourruling houses have emerged up till today in Kuta.
At the time ofAkindele’s settlement at Kuta and for many years that followed, Kuta was independent and not subordinate to any other town, not even Owu his original home. The administration of the town was always in the hand of the ruler. Chieftaincies were created and the Chiefs advised the ruler as to how best the affairs of the town could be run.

At old age when Akindele Anlugbua had lived for many years but could not die, he called his people and told them that he was about to descend into the ground but that they should not forget him by making annual rituals at the very spot where he descended into the ground. Furthermore, he told them that in time of war or any difficuity, they should call on him for assistance and super protection.

Consequent upon this, it just happened one day, that there was a strife between two of his grandchildren. One of them was so enraged that he took a rash decision by taking a whistle and went to the top of an Ose tree to try whether Anlugbuajust made mockery of the promise he gave:hem when he was alive. Calling him by his different names ofAnlugbua of Ogunforiolusere, Olominilefejewe, Okebe-kori-Omotitun, the war has come to destroy all your children. Having heard this, he was hurriedly coming into the town to relief his children of their problems. On the way coming, he ordered the tree and sand by the way side to transform into human beings with guns, bows and arrows on their hands and started the mass killing of the people. Anlughua later picked up the head of one of the decased only to discover that the Own tribal marks (Keke) were on the faces of the deceased ones. He immediately orderd his entourage to withdraw and go back to their respective abodes. Anlugbua also went back to his abode in annoyance and has since refused to come out of the shrine. It has thereafter become an abomination for anybody with ‘Keke' tribal marks to come to his shrine.


UNIQUENESS OF THE SHRINE OF ANLUGBUA


The shrine ofAnlugbua at the forest ofAnlugbua at Kuta, that is the spot where Anlugbua descended into the ground now has a mediumistic marble-rock-like monument or tombstone called ‘Apere’ with a chain coming out of the ground beside the tombstone. This chain could be pulled up to a reasonable distance while Anlugbua usually respond by pulling it back. This is normally done during his annual worship when necessary rights shall be performed at the shrine. Anlugbua also astonishingly climbed up the standing tree at the shrine with the footmarks of the horse being indelibly appearing on the tree from the bottom to its apex where he hanged the “Gaari” the instrument with which he used to climb and control his horse before descending into the ground. Unfortunately the historical tree was not preserved by our people.

, Anlugbua also has a separate spot (shrine) for the worship of ‘Ogun’ (the god of iron) located very close to the tombstone. In anut shell, the non-preservation of the historic ‘tree’not withstaning, the shrine ofAnlugbua is still really worthy of a modern tourist attraction.



DERIVATION OF KUTA

The name Kuta according to oral tradition was derived from an incident that happened to Akindele and his wife. It was said before Akindele left Ipole in searchofhis domain iwth his wife and all instruments of an Oba, there was a terrible internal war among the people of Ipole during which so many great and brave Ozs lost their lives. It was from this war that Akindele managed to escape alongside his pregnnt wife. He thereafter congratulated himself by saying “Iku-Ta-Miti” which means “I escaped death”. This was later abbreviated to “Kuta”

RULING HOUSES AND PRESENTATION OF CANDIDATES
TO FILL THE THRONE OF OLOWU OF KUTA

There are four ruling houses at Kuta viz:

1. Tegbosun Ruling House
2. Delumo Ruling House
3. Adeyi Ruling House and
4. Lanloye Ruling House.

In this respect, there is a government 'Declaration' approved by the Governor in Council on 20th July, 1965 and subsequntly registered on 21st July, 1965. The four ruling houses in the town present candidates in rotation to fill the vacant stool of the Olowu of Kuta. The Ruling House whose turn it is to provide a candidate shall nominate a candidate or candidates at their family meeting and this/these candidate/candidates is/are presented to the kingmakers.

A representative of the Local Government is usually present at meetings of the Ruling House. The Local Government will thereafter make an arrangement whereby the kingmakers would vote for the names of candidates presented by the Ruling House in the presence of the law enforcement agents and a representative of the LocalGoverntnent. The name of the candidate that has the highest number of votes shall be declared Oba-elect and this would be forwarded by the Local Government to the State Government for ratification before the Oba-elect goes to the conclave tagged "Ipebi".

APPOINTMENT OF MAGAJIS


The most elderly male person among members of the Ruling Houses is usually appointed as the Mogaji of the ruling house.

GENERAL INFORMATION

Kuta which now has an estimated population of ninety-thousand is served by many villages which include among others Konko, Olodu, the Railway Station ,Kejo, Arugbode, Egan Aro, Aba Owu and Alagba. Kuta exists along with an adjoining community of Ikoyi which has a separate population and a ruler (the Olukoyi) who is also a member of Kuta Traditional Council. The town has numerous compounds, three Primary Schools, two Nursery Schools, one Arabic School ' and two Secondary Grammar Schools. It also has a Post Office, a Community Bank, a Dispensary, a Maternity Centre and a State Hospital. The three main religion of Islam, Christianity and Traditional worships are practised freely. Farming is the major occupation of the people. However, trading, blacksmithing, dyeing and carpentary are also engaged in.
