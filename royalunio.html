ABOUT OWU KINGDOM


THE ROYAL UNION OF OWU PEOPLE (RUOP) to serve as a forum for unity and
co-operation among Owu People in Nigeria and Diaspora, RUOP is aim at
bringing all Owu People together as a people with a common birth,
heritage and destiny, To foster Unity, Love and Cooperation among
the Owu people, Owu settlements, Owu Obas, Baales, and accredited
community heads.RUOP Membership is open to every person of Owu Ancestry
either born in Nigeria or in Diaspora

THE CONSTITUTION OF THE ROYAL UNION OF OWU PEOPLE

ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE

There shall be an association called THE ROYAL UNION OF OWU PEOPLE (RUOP) to serve as a forum for unity and co-operation among Owu People in Nigeria and Diaspora.
ARTICLE 2: MOTTO OF THE UNION 2.1 The Motto of the Union shall be UNITY, PROGRESS, LOVE AND CO-OPERATION

ARTICLE 3: AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

3.1 To bring all Owu People together as a people with a common birth, heritage and destiny.
3.2 To foster Unity, Love and Cooperation among the Owu people, Owu settlements, Owu Obas, Baales, and accredited community heads.
3.3 To foster and promote the social, economic, industrial and cultural development of the Owu People by encouraging and facilitating the establishment of economic Unity such as Community Banks, Agricultural Development Schemes, Small Scale Industries etc.
3.4 To promote, encourage and recognize the pursuance of excellence among the Owu People. 3.5 To promote and encourage traditionally inherent qualities of integrity, honesty, sense of justice, honour, discipline, good leadership and resoluteness for which the Owu People are known for.
3.6 To strife to promote a common destiny as one people united by birth.
3.7 To do other things that are relevant to the spirit of the union.
3.8 To encourage, motivate, assist, and empower members to achieve a greater height in their chosen business or careers.
ARTICLE 4: MEMBERSHIP

4.1 Membership shall be open to every person of Owu Ancestry either born in Nigeria or in Diaspora.
4.2 In the case of a Settlement or Community claiming Owu Ancestry, the National Executive Council will be mandated to carry out investigations and make its recommendation to the Supreme Council of Owu Obas.
ARTICLE 5: ORGANIZATION

The Royal Union of Owu People (RUOP) shall have three main organs:
5.1 The National Convention
5.2 The Supreme Council of Owu Obas
5.3 The National Executive Council (NEC)
5.1.1 The National Convention shall be a yearly forum at which far reaching decisions of the UNION shall be ratified and shall comprise the following:
5.1.2 All members of Supreme Councils of Owu Obas.
5.1.3 All members of the National Executive Council.
5.1.4 All members from all Owu Settlements and Community in Nigeria and the Diaspora.
5.2.1 The Supreme Council shall be the father of the Union whose responsibility among others are vetting and approval of programmes, policies, budgets etc of the NEC.
5.3.1 National Executive Council (NEC) shall be the policy formulating and
2. implementation arms of the Union after clearance from Council of Obas.
5.3.2 Membership is made up of two nominees from each recognized Zone and each nominees is appointed by the Head of the zone.
ARTICLE 6: SUPREME COUNCIL OF OWU OBAS ETC

6.1 COMPOSITION:
This shall comprise the following:
6.1.1 All Owu Obas
6.1.2 All Owu Baales
6.1.3 All Owu Community Heads

6.1.4 FUNCTIONS

6.1.5 It shall be responsible for the smooth Organization of the Union.
6.1.6 To decide in conjunction with NEC the venue of Annual Convention.
6.1.7 To receive annual report of the NEC at the Convention.
6.1.8 To give final approval to any new Community or Settlement joining the Union.
ARTICLE 7: NATIONAL EXECUTIVE COUNCIL COMPOSITION

7.1 The National Executive Council shall comprise the following:
7.1.1 The President
7.1.2 The Vice President
7.1.3 The Secretary
7.1.4 The Assistant Secretary
7.1.5 The Treasurer
7.1.6 The Financial Secretary
7.1.7 Assistant Financial Secretary
7.1.8 The Publicity/Social Secretary
7.1.9 The Welfare Secretary
7.1.10 The Auditor
7.1.11 The Legal Adviser
7.1.12 The Woman Leader
7.1.13 The Assistant Woman Leader
7.1.14 The Chief Whip
7.1.15 The Youth Leader
7.1.16 The Assistant Youth Leader
7.1.17 The Protocol Officer
7.1.18 The Chairman, Scholarship Board
7.2 FUNCTION

The following shall be the duties and responsibilities of the National Executive Council:
7.2.1 It shall see to the day – to – day running of the Union
7.2.2 To execute all policies and programmes approved by the Supreme Council.
7.2.3 To liaise between the National Convention and the Supreme Council.
7.2.4 To be responsible for the collation and compilation of all reports before and after the Convention.
7.2.5 To organize the Convention
3. 7.2.6 To carry out any other function(s) as may be directed by the Supreme Council from time to time.
ARTICLE 8: DUTIES OF NEC OFFICERS

8.1 THE PRESIDENT

8.1.1 Shall be responsible to the Supreme Council
8.1.2 Shall be responsible for the smoth and effective running of the Union’s Secretariat and activities.
8.1.3 Shall be a signatory to the Union’s Account(s) with its Bankers.
8.2 THE VICE PRESIDENT

8.2.1 Shall assist the President and in his absence shall perform the duties of the President.
8.2.2 Shall be a signatory to the Union’s Account in the absent of the President
8.2.3 Responsible for the empowerment programs.

8.3 SECRETARY

8.3.1 Shall be the custodian of all records and documents of the Union.
8.3.2 Shall records, develop and read minutes of the Union at all meetings.
8.4 ASSISTANT SECRETARY

8.4.1 Shall assist the secretary from time to time
8.4.2 In the absence of the Secretary, he shall perform the duties of the secretary.
8.5 THE NATIONAL TREASURER

8.5.1 Shall receive and lodge into Account of the Union all monies received on behalf of the Union.
8.5.2 Shall be a signatory to the Union’s Account(s) with its Bankers.
8.5.3 Shall together with the Financial Secretary be responsible for the preparation of all periodical account.
8.5.4 Shall present the account as often desired to the National Executive Council and the Supreme Council as required by the Union.
8.5.5 To supervise the preparation of annual budget.
8.6 THE NATIONAL FINANCIAL SECRETARY

8.6.1 Shall be responsible for collecting all monies and keeping the records of all monies so collected on behalf of the Union.
8.6.2 Shall deliver to the Treasurer before the end of the meetings, monies collected on behalf of the Union.
8.6.3 Shall together with the Treasurer be responsible for the preparation of all periodical Accounts of the Union.
8.7 ASSISTANT FINANCIAL SECRETARY

8.7.1 Shall assist the Financial Secretary in carry out his duties.
8.7.2 Shall in the absence of the Financial Secretary, perform his duties.
8.7.3 Shall carry out any duties that might be assigned to him by the President.
8.8 PUBLICITY AND SOCIAL SECRETARY

8.8.1 Shall be responsible for all social and publicity activities of the Union.
8.8.2 Shall be involved in the preparation of the Annual Convention.
8.8.3 Shall carry out other duties that may be assigned to him by the President.
4. 8.9 WELFARE SECRETARY

8.9.1 Shall monitor the welfare of the Organs of the Union.
8.9.2 Shall carry out duties assigned by the President.
8.10 AUDITOR

8.10.1 Shall be responsible for the auditing of the Union’s Account(s)
8.10.2 Shall also report to the NEC and the Supreme Council of Owu Obas.
8.11 LEGAL ADVISER

8.11.1 Shall handle all legal matters of the Union.
8.11.2 Shall advise the NEC on legal issues affecting the Union.
8.12 WOMAN LEADER

8.12.1 Shall coordinate the activities of women in the Union.
8.12.2 Shall mobilize women in Owu Settlement.
8.12.3 Shall work towards motivation and economic empowerment of all Owu women.
8.12.4 Shall carry out duties and responsibilities that might be assigned to her by the President.
8.13 ASSISTANT WOMAN LEADER

8.13.1 To assist the woman leader and to perform the duties of woman leader in absent.
ARTICLE 9: NATIONAL CONVENTION

9.1 The National Convention of the Union shall hold in the first weekend of December of every year at a venue to be decided by the Supreme Council in conjunction with the NEC.
9.2 The National Convention shall be presided over by the current Chairman of the Supreme Council of Owu Obas.
9.3 Two days (Friday and Saturday) shall be reserved for the National Convention.
9.4 The National Convention shall be:
9.4.1 The venue to receive the report of Committees on special assignment and to receive and consider Accounts and all other reports.
9.4.2 The venue for the ratification of new committee seeking membership of the Union.
9.4.3 The occasion for the presentation of Awards and recognition to deserving members and non members of the Union.
9.4.4 The venue for the ratification of any amendment(s) to the Constitution.
9.4.5 The venue to socialize; interact and display Owu Cultural heritage.
ARTICLE 10: TENURE OF OFFICE

10.1 SUPREME COUNCIL

10.1.1 The Owu Supreme Council shall be headed by a Chairman who shall hold office for one calendar year.
10.1.2 The Chairman shall be the Owu Oba in whose domain the next convention of the Union is to be held.
10.1.3 His tenure shall commence immediately after the convention preceding the one to be held in his domain and shall end after the said convention.
10.1.4 He shall be responsible for hosting the Council Meeting(s) preceding the Convention. His is to allow the Chairman to brief the Council on arrangement for the up-coming Convention and other relevant issues of policy or importance.
5. 10.2 NATIONAL EXECUTIVE COUNCIL

10.2.1 The National Executive Council shall be headed by a President who shall hold office for two (2) years.
10.2.2 The President and all elected officers of NEC are free to stand for re-election for a final term of 2 years.
10.2.3 This tenure of office shall also apply to the zonal Offices.

ARTICLE 11: ZONAL ORGANIZATION

11.1 The Union shall be organized on Zonal basis.
11.2 There shall be established for each of the Settlement or Community of the Royal Union of Owu People a Zonal Secretariat to be headed by a Zonal Chairman.
11.3 Shall be responsible to the Head of such Settlement or Community.
11.4 Zonal Officers shall include:-

a. Chairman
b. Secretary
c. Deputy Secretary
d. Treasurer
e. Financial Secretary
f. Pulicity and Welfare Secretary
11.5 Zonal Chairman shall be elected by the Community.
11.6 The Zonal Chairman shall be responsible to the Head of the Community.
ARTICLE 12: ELECTION OF OFFICERS

12.1 There shall be an election into the various offices of the Union (National/Zone) once every two years.
12.2 Any member of RUOP at the Executive and Zonal levels shall be eligible to stand for effective offices provided they meet the criteria that may be spelt out by the Supreme Council.
12.3 The above notwithstanding, anybody standing for such elective positions must have been an active and financial member of RUOP for a minimum of two years preceding such election.
12.4 Anybody standing for election must have been duly selected by his Zonal Head as a representative.
12.5 Election shall be by secret ballot.
12.6 Election shall be decided based on a simple majority.
ARTICLE 13: THE SECRETARIAT

13.1 The Temporary Secretariat of the Union shall be located in Owu Kingdom Abeokuta, Ogun State.
13.2 The Royal Union of Owu People’s Secretariat shall be headed by the National Secretary.
ARTICLE 14: FINANCE

14.1 The Account(s) of the Union shall be kept in Abeokuta in one or two reputable Banks.
14.2 Each Zone shall pay an Annual Subscription of Six Thousand Naira (6,000.00) or as may be decided by the Supreme Council from time to time.
14.3 It shall be the responsibility of all members including NEC to source for donations and or gifts for the Union within or outside the Union.
6. 14.4 The financial year of the Union shall run from January to December of every year.
14.5 There shall be three (3) signatories to the Union’s Account. Any two of the signatories can withdraw from the Accounts of the Union. The three signatories shall be the President, Vice President, and Treasurer.
14.6 Each zone shall pay a special levy to meet the expenses of the annual convention of the Union. The current levy is Twenty Thousand Naira per zone and this can be increase from time to time as agreed by the Supreme Council.
14.7 Any other source or sources of fund that Supreme Council may approve from time to time.
ARTICLE 15: DISCIPLINE

15.1 There shall be a disciplinary committee of 5 members to be charred by the Legal Adviser
15.2 The Committee will consider and investigate all charges or reports(s) that may be brought against any member(s) of RUOP.
15.3 The person to be investigated will be given the opportunities of being head.
15.4 The Committee shall submit its findings or recommendation within a fortnight (Two weeks) to the present who will ensure that the recommendation or findings of the committee are carry out.
15.5 Any affected person who is not happy about the findings or recommendations of the committee may appeal to the Supreme Council of Owu Obas, Baales, and Community Heads.
15.6 The Supreme Council shall consider the findings or recommendations of the committee and give its own decisions on the issue.
15.7 The decisions of the Supreme Council of Obas etc. shall be final on the issues.
15.8 indictable offence(s) in this respect shall include all acts of Omission or Commission that may bring dishonour and or stain to the name and integrity of the Union.
15.9 The sanction(s) in respect of any issue will be as recommended by the committee and confirmed or varied by the Supreme Council.
ARTICLE 16: MEETINGS

16.1 SCHEDULE OF MEETINGS

16.1.1 The Council of Obas, Baales, and Community Heads shall meet 2 times in a year; June and December, Notwithstanding, the Supreme Council has the right to call for emergency or extra ordinary meeting.
16.1.2 The joint meeting of the Supreme Council and National Executive shall hold in June, September, December. The dates and venue shall be determined by the current Chairman of the Obas.
16.1.3 The join meeting of the Supreme Council/NEC shall hold in June, September and December and past President of the union should be present at the meeting.
16.1.4 NEC meetings shall be held monthly at dates and venues to be determined by NEC. This provision is without prejudice to any emergency meetings.
16.1.5 Zonal meetings shall be held monthly at the dates and venue to be determined as their circumstances permit.
16.2 NOTICE OF MEETINGS

16.2.1 Adequate and proper notices of regular meetings shall be given to members. At least a period of one week must exist between the date of receipt of notice by a member and proposed date of meeting.
7. 16.2.2 In case of emergency meeting(s), the adequacy of notice shall be judged on whether or not there is any urgent need for the meeting.
16.2.3 The quorum at any meeting of the Union shall be at least one-third (1/3) of the number of persons eligible to attend such a meeting.
16.2.4 A special session of the Supreme Council and NEC may hold on a day before the convention to discuss and determine important matters affecting the Union.
ARTICLE 17: AMENDMENT TO CONSTITUTION

17.1 Any amendment to the constitution shall be proposed and supported by at least Three zonal chapters and dispatched to reach the President at least three months before the Convention date for the year.
17.2 The President shall first present the proposed amendment(s) to the meeting of the National Executive Council.
17.3 The National Executive Council will determine whether such Amendment are desirable before it is presented to the Supreme Council for approval and presentation at the next Convention.
17.4 INTERPRETATION
17.5 ZONE: Only an Owu Settlement shall be qualified to be recognized as a zone.
17.6 UNITY: Owu indigenes residing in any part of the world are free to come together as a meeting unit while still retaining membership of the zone of their respective settlement. Such a unit may opt to be members of a recognized zone nearest to their places of abode orresident. At no time can a Meeting Unit claim the status of a zone except in special cases as members in the Diaspora or as may be approved by the Supreme Council.
